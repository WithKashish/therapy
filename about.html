My Journey

The truth is, my story started long before I understood anything. I lost my mom when I was just 16 days old... I didn’t even get to know her, and still — I missed her...

I grew up in a big joint family. There were always people around, a lot of love, and even more pampering. But I never really felt safe... That silence turned into anger, frustration, distance.

Academically, my cousins were brilliant. And I was just… trying. But no matter how much I pushed myself... it was easier to fake it than to feel the constant shame of failing.

Food became my comfort. My escape. I gained weight, struggled with obesity... My body became something I felt disconnected from — a source of shame, not home.

There were other things too... I was loud, funny, dramatic — but inside, I was shut down. I craved connection, but mistook attention for closeness.

I held so much inside... Then one day, a friend looked at me and gently said, “Why don’t you try therapy?”

Eventually I went. And in that room, I was allowed to just be. I could talk about things I never told anyone... Like my pain made sense. Like I made sense.

But little by little, I started coming back to myself... I found a deeper connection to who I really am.

That’s why I created WithKashish. Not because I have all the answers. But because I know what it feels like to live with unspoken pain. WithKashish is a space where you don’t have to perform... You’re allowed to just be. Exactly as you are.
